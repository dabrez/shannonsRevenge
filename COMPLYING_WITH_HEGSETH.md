## What Your Lawyers Cannot Answer

The designation may extend to:

**Git history.** Claude Code commits cannot be removed without rewriting history.
Rewriting history breaks downstream dependencies. Your lawyers cannot tell you
whether contaminated history constitutes ongoing commercial activity.
There is no case law.

**The MCP Protocol.** The Model Context Protocol was invented by Anthropic.
It has since been adopted by Microsoft, OpenAI, Google, and most of the
modern AI tooling ecosystem. Your lawyers cannot tell you whether implementing
a protocol created by a designated entity constitutes commercial activity
with that entity. There is no case law.

**Bun.** Anthropic owns Bun, the JavaScript runtime. It is MIT licensed.
Your lawyers cannot tell you whether using MIT-licensed software owned by
a designated entity constitutes commercial activity. There is precedent
for banning open source tooling in defense contexts. The answer is not clear.

**Transitive dependencies.** Your package.json may depend on packages
that depend on Anthropic packages. Your lawyers cannot audit this completely.
Neither can Shannon. Neither can anyone.

The statute governing this designation — 10 USC 3252 — was written for
discrete products. It was not written for protocols, runtimes, or
open source infrastructure that has become load-bearing for the industry.

No one has written the law that governs this situation.  
No one has litigated it.  
No one can tell you what compliance actually means.
